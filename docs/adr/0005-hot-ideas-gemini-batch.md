# Hot Ideas: free-Gemini batch generation, with an absolute-metric display sort

Each Hot idea and its Blueprint is generated **server-side with free Gemini** (`gemini-2.5-flash`), in **batch**, stored in the DB, and refreshed on a cadence by the freshness scheduler (see ADR 0004) — never generated per page view. We chose free Gemini over paid Gemini or Claude because the volume (~1,200 ideas × a full Blueprint each) makes per-call cost the binding constraint, and the output is descriptive build-planning where flash-tier quality is sufficient; the only ceiling is the free rate limit, which the paced sweep respects (slice per boot, ~7-day full cycle, never blocking).

**Source-app selection gate** (which apps earn an idea): a blend of Rising + recently-Released + low-hanging-fruit (high Revenue estimate, low Rating, decent review volume), *not* pure Growth score — because Snapshot history is thin (3 days) and growth signal is noisy. Growth weight increases automatically as history accrues. This was confirmed by inspecting the live appkittie.com Hot Ideas surface (2026-06-10): its user-facing **sort metrics are all absolute** — `Created · Released · Reviews · Downloads · Revenue · Rating · Price` — with **no growth/momentum sort at all**. So "fast-growing" is only the backend selection gate; the displayed ranking never depends on Snapshot history, which removes the thin-data blocker.

We clone that exact surface: the seven absolute sort metrics plus Blueprint filter toggles (Needs backend / Needs database / Needs AI). The per-idea **About narrative** is the one deliberate exception to batch generation — lazy-on-view, one Gemini call on first open, cached forever (descriptive, not time-sensitive, and only apps actually viewed cost a call).
